The Low-Friction Out-of-Network Guide

Your care shouldn't be dictated by an insurance auditor who doesn't know the difference between RSD and general anxiety. This guide lowers the executive function hurdle for accessing specialist care when you're out-of-network.

Why Out-of-Network Is a Privacy and Quality Safeguard

The Autonomy Filter: When you work with an out-of-network provider, insurance companies have less control over your treatment plan, session frequency, and diagnostic framing. For 2SLGBTQI+ and neurodivergent clients, this means:

  1. Your diagnoses aren't weaponized in future insurance applications
  2. Your therapy isn't subject to arbitrary session limits based on outdated clinical guidelines
  3. Your Internal Courtroom stays between you and Joshua—not you, Joshua, and a claims adjuster in Des Moines
The Bottom Line

We handle the litigation with your insurance company so you can focus on the territory reclamation. You pay your co-insurance upfront. We submit the superbill. Your insurance reimburses you directly.

The 3-Step Thrizer Process (Low Executive Function Edition)

1.

Check Your Benefits (Before Your Intake)

Use Thrizer to verify your out-of-network mental health benefits:

Visit eligibility.thrizer.com/facility/thrizer7t1oh

What You'll Learn:

  1. Your out-of-network deductible (how much you pay before reimbursement kicks in)
  2. Your co-insurance percentage (typically 50-80% reimbursement after deductible)
  3. Your out-of-pocket maximum (the cap on your annual spending)

Example: If your session is $150, your deductible is met, and your co-insurance is 70%, you pay $150 upfront and get $105 back from insurance. Your actual cost per session: $45.

2.

Book Your Intake and Pay

Dignity Investment, Not a Transaction:

You'll pay $225 for your intake session via Carepatron. This isn't a 50-minute hour—it's an Expert Witness in the trial of your own life who already knows the laws of the Double-Outsider economy.

Payment Methods Accepted: Credit/debit cards, HSA/FSA cards.

3.

We Generate Your Superbill (You Submit It)

Zero Cognitive Load for You:

After each session, we generate a Superbill—a standardized receipt with all the codes your insurance needs. You'll receive it via email within 48 hours.

How to Submit Your Superbill:

  1. Log into your insurance portal (or use their mobile app)
  2. Find "Submit a Claim" or "Out-of-Network Reimbursement"
  3. Upload the superbill PDF (no forms to fill out—the superbill has everything)
  4. Wait 2-4 weeks for your reimbursement check or direct deposit

If You Get Stuck: Email Joshua@queerpathways.org with the subject line "Superbill Support" and we'll walk you through it or provide a pre-filled claim form template.

Common Out-of-Network Questions

What if my insurance denies the claim?

Rare, but it happens. We provide a Letter of Medical Necessity template you can submit with your appeal. For clients with complex diagnoses (ADHD + PTSD, for example), we can also provide clinical justification for why specialist care is medically necessary.

Can I use my HSA/FSA card?

Yes. You can pay directly with your HSA/FSA card at the time of service, which means you're using pre-tax dollars. You don't need to submit a superbill to get reimbursed—the HSA/FSA automatically covers qualified mental health services.

What if I can't afford the upfront cost?

Email Joshua@queerpathways.org. We offer a limited number of sliding scale slots for clients in active financial crisis. The investment shouldn't be the wall preventing you from getting support.

Do you take Medicare or Medicaid?

Not at this time. Queer Pathways is structured as a specialist practice with a focus on high-complexity, identity-fluent care that requires clinical autonomy outside of Medicare/Medicaid constraints.
