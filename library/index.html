About Joshua Jonassaint, LCSW

Most therapy is designed to "fix" what is broken. It operates on a deficit model: a diagnostic ledger where you are the debt and the clinician is the auditor. At Queer Pathways, we have burned that ledger.

Browse the Resource Library →

I am Joshua Jonassaint (They/Them), and I don't believe you are broken. I believe you are unmapped. My work isn't about restoration to a "normal" that was never built for you; it is about territory reclamation. It is about taking back the psychological and somatic ground that systemic oppression, trauma, and shame have occupied for far too long.

As a licensed clinical social worker providing telehealth therapy across Pennsylvania, my role is not to be your judge or your mechanic. I am an architect and a guide. We are here to survey the landscape of your life, identify the occupied territories, and build a sanctuary where your sovereignty is the only law.

The Internal Trial: Why You're Reading This

You carry an Internal Courtroom. It litigates your every decision, cross-examines your worthiness, and keeps a meticulous Evidence Locker of every perceived failure. The Internal Prosecutor has been building a case against you for years, cataloging every mistake, every "too much" or "not enough," every moment you didn't meet an impossible standard.

That court has been in session for far too long. At Queer Pathways, the first thing we do is adjourn it.

This isn't about silencing your inner voice or pretending harm didn't happen. It's about recognizing that the courtroom itself is the problem—a system imposed by a world that was never designed for you. We don't litigate your worth here. We reclaim the territory that shame, trauma, and systemic oppression have occupied. We burn the ledger and map new ground.

Beyond "Affirming": The Power of Identity-Fluent Care

In the mental health world, "LGBTQ-affirming" has become a buzzword: a participation trophy for clinicians who know what the acronym stands for. For the high-capacity queer professional, the polycule navigating complex attachment, or the kink practitioner seeking somatic depth, "affirming" is the bare minimum. It's the 101 education you shouldn't have to provide to your own therapist.

I offer identity-fluent care.

This means you don't have to explain the vocabulary of your life. Whether we are discussing the nuances of minority stress, the communication architecture of a triad, or the somatic power dynamics of a scene, I speak the language natively. We skip the definitions and go straight to the work. My practice is rooted in the lived experience of the 2SLGBTQI+ community, backed by high-level clinical training and WPATH certification.

The Integrated Pleasure Model: Reclaiming Joy as Resistance

Traditional therapy often treats pleasure as a secondary concern: a reward you get once you've finished "processing" your trauma. I see pleasure as the primary fuel for the reclamation process.

Through the Integrated Pleasure Model, we move pleasure from the periphery to the center of the clinical picture. Shame is a "garbage coat" that the world forces you to wear; pleasure is the mechanism that helps you shrug it off.

We aren't just looking for the absence of pain. We are looking for the presence of vitality. For clients in the kink and BDSM communities, this means integrating somatic power dynamics and sensory play into the healing journey. We treat your desires not as symptoms to be managed, but as maps to your most authentic self.

The Wabi-Sabi Body Framework: Finding Sovereignty in the Seams

The world demands perfection, especially from marginalized bodies. We are told to be smaller, quieter, more "palatable."

The Wabi-Sabi Body Framework is the answer to that demand. Rooted in the Japanese aesthetic of finding beauty in imperfection and impermanence, this framework treats your history—including your scars, your transitions, and your neurodivergence—as the "golden joinery" of your life.

In our sessions, we look at your body not as a project to be completed, but as a site of Somatic Sovereignty. We use practices and tools to help you inhabit your skin without the constant interference of an internal auditor. We repair the "leaky buckets" of your energy and teach you how to hold your own space with authority.

Clinical Advocacy: The Therapist as Collaborator

I am a fierce advocate. My approach is direct, trauma-informed, and unapologetically queer. I recognize that the Internal Courtroom many of us carry—that voice that litigates our every move—is often just a reflection of the systemic impact of the world outside.

My job is to help you adjourn that court.

When you work with me, you aren't just getting a listener. You are getting a strategist. Whether we are uncovering your attachment style to repair a relationship or navigating the high-torque demands of a professional career in Pennsylvania, we work in tandem. I use multi-channel prompts to keep the administrative scaffolding of therapy invisible. Your bandwidth is precious. I respect it by ensuring logistics are seamless, so your focus stays on the reclamation.

Who This Space Is For

Queer Pathways is a specialized sanctuary. It is designed for those who are tired of being the "teacher" in the therapy room.

  1. Queer Professionals in PA. You are high-capacity, high-impact, and looking for a therapist who understands the specific weight of navigating professional spaces while queer.
  2. Polycules & ENM Practitioners. Whether you are in a triad, a quad, or a solo-poly explorer, we provide structured support for boundaries, communication, and the complex blueprints of non-traditional connection.
  3. The Kink & BDSM Community. We provide a space where your leather, your toys, and your archetypes are respected as valid tools for somatic and relational growth.

The Court Is Adjourned

There is a specific kind of exhaustion that comes from living in a world that views your existence as a debate. In this practice, the debate is over.

"The court is adjourned here; there is no 'broken,' only 'unmapped.'"

This is the foundational promise of Queer Pathways. We aren't here to find out what's "wrong" with you. We are here to map the territory you've already conquered and design the life you actually want to live.

You have spent enough time litigating your own worth. It's time to stop explaining and start evolving.

How It Works

  1. Schedule a free 15-minute consultation to determine if we're a fit. No pressure, no sales pitch—just clarity.
  2. Sessions are conducted via telehealth across Pennsylvania. Meet from your own sanctuary, wherever that is.
  3. Sliding scale and insurance options available. Your financial reality shouldn't be a barrier to care. We'll discuss pricing during the consultation.
  4. No long-term commitment required. You control the pace. This is your reclamation, not a contract.
